# Philosophy

This document explains why this tool exists and what it is designed to encourage.

It is intentionally opinionated.

---

## The Problem

Many engineering organizations struggle with:

- Opaque performance evaluation
- Political signaling
- Subjective manager interpretation
- Inability to quickly detect disengagement
- Hidden underperformance

When contribution signals are invisible, politics fills the gap. **Transparency reduces politics.**

---

## What We Value

This tool encodes a specific set of values:

- Frequent shipping
- Small, mergeable PRs
- Fast feedback cycles
- High review participation
- Visible ownership
- Sustained contribution over time

We believe:

- Smaller PRs are healthier than large monoliths.
- Fast merges reduce risk.
- High review engagement improves system coherence.
- Throughput matters.

This is **NOT** a universal definition of engineering excellence. It **IS** a deliberate cultural choice.

---

## Transparency Over Private Scoring

Private evaluation systems erode trust.

This tool is designed to be:

- Public
- Inspectable
- Critique-friendly
- Open source

Everyone can see:

- The formula
- The weights
- The output

If incentives exist, they should be visible.

---

## Competitive but Cooperative

We believe high-performing teams can be:

- Competitive internally
- Cooperative externally
- Unified around shared goals

Like elite sports teams:

- Everyone wants the team to win.
- Top performers still push themselves.
- Contribution is visible.

This tool reinforces that dynamic.

---

## Roles and Evaluation Context

Not all engineers are evaluated identically.

### Developers

Expected to:
- Maintain high throughput
- Review consistently
- Keep merge cycles tight
- Contribute visibly

### Architects

Expected to:
- Do all of the above
- Shape 1–2 year system direction
- Reduce long-term complexity
- Maintain technical involvement
- Provide leverage across the system

Architects are judged on their ability to maintain long-term impact, while achieving continued high throughput.

---

## What This Tool Does Not Measure

This tool does not directly measure:

- Architectural elegance
- Refactor depth
- Mentorship impact
- System simplification
- Long-horizon leverage

**Human judgment remains essential.**

---

## On Burnout and Sustainability

We believe burnout is driven primarily by:

- Politics
- Role ambiguity
- Hidden evaluation
- Excessive workload without autonomy

Transparency reduces those risks.

This tool does **NOT** require excessive hours.
It **DOES** require visible contribution.

Intensity must be paired with leadership tone and sustainable pacing.

---

## On Underperformance

Low contribution patterns are visible.

**This is intentional.**

We optimize for performance density.
We allow self-selection.
We prefer clarity to ambiguity.

---

## Scaling Considerations

This model works best when:

- Leadership maintains calibration
- Hiring bar is high
- Architectural authority is strong
- Code quality is enforced

No metric system substitutes for leadership quality.

It amplifies whatever culture exists.

---

## Final Thought

**This tool encodes what we value.**

If you value the same things, use it.
If you don’t, adapt it.

The formula is adjustable.
The philosophy is transparent.
